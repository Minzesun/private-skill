# 中南大学研究生学位论文格式规范

Use this reference when the user asks for a Central South University
(`中南大学`, `CSU`) graduate thesis, dissertation, Word/DOCX thesis template,
or formatting based on `中大研字〔2022〕8号` / `中南大学研究生学位论文撰写规范`.

This reference is extracted from the 2022-04-14 school-meeting version of
`中南大学研究生学位论文撰写规范`, issued on 2022-04-18. It overrides the generic
`chinese_phd_thesis_985` preset whenever the target university is 中南大学.

## Document-wide requirements

- Use Word 2013 or later.
- Paper: A4.
- Output print area: 240 mm x 146 mm, including the header.
- Set page margins to reproduce that print area: left 3.2 cm, right 3.2 cm,
  top 2.85 cm, bottom 2.85 cm.
- Header distance from top edge: 1.5 cm.
- Footer distance from bottom edge: 1.75 cm.
- Print duplex.
- Use simplified Chinese for normal theses. Foreign-language theses must include
  Chinese title/abstract annotations and a detailed Chinese abstract of 5000
  characters when required by the regulation.
- Master thesis length: normally at least 20,000 Chinese characters.
- Doctoral thesis length: normally at least 50,000 Chinese characters.
- The length count excludes Chinese/English abstracts, references, appendices,
  and acknowledgements.

## Required content order

Build the template in this order, with optional sections retained as clearly
marked placeholders:

1. Cover and title page.
2. Originality statement and copyright authorization.
3. Chinese abstract.
4. English abstract.
5. Table of contents.
6. English abbreviation explanation, required for medical theses.
7. Symbol explanation, when needed.
8. Main text.
9. References.
10. Review article, required for medical theses.
11. Appendices, when needed.
12. Main research achievements during the degree.
13. Acknowledgements.

## Cover, spine, and title page

- Cover variants: public academic degree, public professional degree, classified
  academic degree, and classified professional degree.
- Classified covers must mark secrecy level and classification date in the upper
  right corner after school secrecy-office approval/stamp.
- The cover front must include thesis title, first-level discipline or
  professional degree category, second-level discipline or professional field,
  author name, and supervisor name.
- The spine must show `博（或硕）士学位论文` at the top and `中南大学` at the bottom.
- The title page is unified and does not include a secrecy-level field.
- Title-page variants: academic degree and professional degree.
- The title page must include all cover-front fields plus Chinese Library
  Classification number, UDC, school code, degree type, subtitle when needed,
  research direction, second-level training unit, co-supervisor when needed,
  defense date, and defense committee chair.
- The title page must be a separate page printed on a right-hand page.
- Thesis title must summarize the most important content, be precise and
  specific, and normally stay within 25 Chinese characters.
- Do not use a subtitle on the cover.
- First-level discipline or professional degree category must follow the 2018
  State Council discipline catalogue.
- Research direction should be concise keyword phrases.
- Supervisor name must match the approved enrollment supervisor. Only one
  supervisor is allowed. If needed, list only one co-supervisor on the title page.

## Originality and copyright statement

- Place the originality statement and copyright authorization on one standalone
  page after the title page.
- Use the official wording when provided by the school or college. If not
  provided, insert protected placeholders rather than inventing binding legal
  text.

## Chinese abstract

- Start a new page.
- Leave one blank line at the top.
- Center the thesis title: SimHei, 16 pt, bold, 18 pt before, 12 pt after.
- On the next line, start from the left margin with `摘要：`: SimHei, 14 pt,
  bold.
- Abstract body: SimSun, 14 pt; justified; first-line indent 2 Chinese
  characters.
- Line spacing: exactly 20 pt.
- Content should state research purpose, methods, achievements, and conclusions;
  highlight creative results and new insights; use concise language.
- Master Chinese abstract: about 500 Chinese characters.
- Doctoral Chinese abstract: about 1000 Chinese characters.
- After the abstract body, leave one blank line and list the number of figures,
  tables, and references: SimSun, 14 pt.
- Leave two blank lines, then write `关键字：` and `分类号：`: SimHei, 14 pt,
  bold.
- Choose 3-8 keywords.
- Separate Chinese keywords with full-width semicolons and do not add punctuation
  after the last keyword.

## English abstract

- Start a new page.
- Leave one blank line at the top.
- Center the English title: Times New Roman, 16 pt, bold.
- On the next line, start from the left margin with `Abstract:`: Times New
  Roman, 14 pt, bold.
- English abstract body: Times New Roman, 14 pt; justified; first-line indent
  equivalent to 2 characters.
- Line spacing: exactly 20 pt.
- Leave two blank lines after the body.
- Write `Keywords:` and `Classification:` from the left margin: Times New Roman,
  14 pt, bold.
- Separate English keywords with half-width semicolons and do not add
  punctuation after the last keyword.

## Table of contents

- The table of contents must list chapter titles, first-level section titles,
  second-level section titles, and their page numbers.
- Use Roman page numbers for front matter beginning at the Chinese abstract.
- Use continuous Arabic page numbers for the main text and back matter.
- Heading text: `目  录`, with two spaces between characters; SimHei, 16 pt,
  bold; 18 pt before; 12 pt after.
- Leave one blank line above and below the contents heading.
- Chapter/section/subsection numbering defaults to `1`, `1.1`, `1.1.1`.
- Humanities and social-science theses may use `第一章`, `第一节`, `一、`,
  `（一）`.
- Connect titles and page numbers with dotted leaders.
- Contents chapter entries: SimHei, 12 pt.
- Other contents entries: SimSun, 12 pt.
- Line spacing: exactly 20 pt.

## English abbreviations and symbols

- Medical theses must include an English abbreviation explanation before the
  main text.
- Symbol explanations are required when many physical quantity symbols, marks,
  abbreviations, special measurement units, custom nouns, or terms appear.
- If such items are few, explain them at first mention instead of making a
  separate list.
- Symbol heading: `符号说明`, centered; SimHei, 16 pt, bold; 18 pt before;
  12 pt after; leave one blank line above.
- Use a table for explanations. Format table text according to thesis table
  rules.

## Main text

- The main text must start on a new right-hand page.
- Each chapter starts on a new page.
- Typical components: numbered headings, narrative text, figures, tables, and
  formulas.
- Possible chapter types include introduction/review, theory, calculation
  method, experimental apparatus and testing method, result analysis and
  discussion, research achievements, conclusion and outlook.
- Use real Word heading styles and numbering; do not fake headings by direct
  formatting.
- Chapter number is centered; section and subsection numbers start at the left
  margin.
- Use a middle dot between heading levels, e.g. `2.1`; do not add a trailing dot
  after the final number.
- Put one Chinese-character space between the number and title.
- Chapter title occupies one line; body begins on the next line.
- Body paragraphs: first-line indent 2 Chinese characters.
- Chinese body text: SimSun, 12 pt.
- English body text: Times New Roman, 12 pt.
- Character spacing: standard.
- Chapter title: SimHei, 16 pt, bold; centered; 18 pt before; 12 pt after.
- Section title: SimSun, 14 pt; left aligned; 10 pt before; 8 pt after.
- Subsection title: SimSun, 12 pt; left aligned; 10 pt before; 8 pt after.
- Body and all heading levels use exactly 20 pt line spacing, except paragraphs
  containing formulas when mathematical expression requires another spacing.
- Do not insert blank lines between section titles, subsection titles, and body.
- After `1.1.1`, deeper headings may use `1.1.1.1`.
- Ordered body content sequence: `（1）` first-level items, `①` second-level
  items, `a` third-level items, and `（a）` fourth-level items.

## Figures

- Number figures by chapter or continuously with Arabic numerals, e.g. `图2-5`.
- Figure captions go below figures.
- Do not leave a blank line between a figure and its caption.
- Chinese figure caption: KaiTi, 10.5 pt.
- English figure caption: Times New Roman, 10.5 pt.
- Caption alignment: centered.
- Caption spacing: 0 lines before, 1 line after.
- Leave one space between figure number and figure title.
- Subfigures use `（a）`, `（b）`, `（c）`; add subfigure titles when needed.
- When referencing subfigures in text, cite with numbering such as `图5-7-a`.
- Terms, symbols, and units inside figures must match the main text.

## Tables

- Number tables by chapter or continuously with Arabic numerals, e.g. `表3-2`.
- Table captions go above tables.
- Do not leave a blank line between a table caption and its table.
- Chinese table caption: KaiTi, 10.5 pt.
- English table caption: Times New Roman, 10.5 pt.
- Caption alignment: centered.
- Caption spacing: 1 line before, 0 lines after.
- Leave one space between table number and table title.
- Table Chinese text: SimSun, 10.5 pt.
- Table English text: Times New Roman, 10.5 pt.
- Table line spacing: single.
- Table cell text must be horizontally and vertically centered unless a column
  clearly requires another alignment for readability.
- Parameters in tables must mark quantity and unit symbols.
- Use three-line tables as the default: no vertical rules and no regular
  internal gridlines.
- Top and bottom rules: 1.5 pt.
- Header separator rule: 0.75 pt.
- Repeated continuation tables must repeat table headers. If a table continues
  on following pages, repeat the table number and title followed by `续` above
  the continuation.

## Formulas

- Number formulas with Arabic numerals in parentheses, e.g. `（5-1）`.
- Formula text uses the same size as body text.
- Use one uniform formula layout throughout the thesis: either centered formulas
  or formulas in a new paragraph with 2-character indentation.
- Put formula numbers at the right end of the line.
- Do not use dotted leaders between formulas and their numbers.
- If formulas must be separated into variants, use `a`, `b`, `c`, e.g.
  `（3-1-a）`, `（3-1-b）`.

## Notes under figures, tables, or formulas

- If a figure, table, or formula has notes, write `附注` immediately below it.
- `附注`: KaiTi, 10.5 pt.
- Indent the note by 2 Chinese characters.
- Leave one blank line after the note before continuing body text.
- Use single line spacing between figures, tables, formulas, and surrounding
  body text.

## References

- Only include sources directly read by the author and cited in the text.
- Place references at the end of the main text, not after each chapter.
- Follow GB/T 7714-2015.
- Humanities and social-science theses may use footnotes for additional
  explanations of keywords, points, or statements.
- Follow academic ethics and avoid plagiarism.
- For medical theses, references in the main thesis are normally at least 80
  for doctoral theses and at least 50 for master theses, unless the degree
  committee approves an exception for rare diseases, emerging diseases, new
  technologies, inventions, or similar topics.
- References may use numeric order or author-year style. Numeric order is
  recommended.
- Humanities and social-science theses may organize references by literature
  type according to disciplinary conventions.
- Author names normally list the first three authors, followed by `，等` or
  `，et al.`.
- Business theses may list all authors when business journals normally have no
  more than five authors.
- Reference heading: `参考文献`, centered; SimHei, 16 pt, bold; 18 pt before;
  12 pt after; leave one blank line above.
- Reference body starts at the left margin.
- Reference label format: `[1]`.
- Chinese references: SimSun, 12 pt.
- English references: Times New Roman, 12 pt.
- Use half-width punctuation for all reference symbols.
- Include examples for monographs, monograph chapters, serial publications,
  serial-article chapters, patents, and electronic resources when making a
  template.

## Medical review article

- Medical theses must include one review article.
- The review consists of title, Chinese abstract with keywords, body, and
  references.
- The review topic and content should align with the thesis research direction.
- Review Chinese abstract: no more than 500 Chinese characters.
- Review keywords: 4-7.
- Review body may be divided into chapters. The first chapter is `前言`; the
  last chapter is `总结`.
- Medical doctoral review body: normally at least 5000 Chinese characters.
- Medical master review body: normally at least 3000 Chinese characters.
- Review reference-count requirements match the main thesis unless the degree
  committee approves an exception.
- If details are unclear, follow the main-text formatting rules.

## Appendices

- Appendices include derivations too long for the main text, auxiliary
  mathematical tools, supporting tables, repeated data charts, code, and
  explanations.
- Name appendices in order: `附录A`, `附录B`, etc.
- Each appendix must have a title.
- Leave two spaces between appendix sequence and appendix title, e.g.
  `附录A  关于……的说明`.
- Appendix heading: centered; SimHei, 16 pt, bold; 18 pt before; 12 pt after;
  leave one blank line above and below.
- Appendix paragraphs indent 2 Chinese characters.
- Appendix figures, tables, and formulas use separate numbering with appendix
  letters, e.g. `图A-1`, `表B-2`, `式（C-3）`.

## Research achievements during the degree

- Heading: `攻读学位期间主要的研究成果`, centered; SimHei, 16 pt, bold;
  18 pt before; 12 pt after; leave one blank line above and below.
- List degree-period research achievements related to the thesis in chronological
  order and by category.
- Categories may include research projects, awards, patents, monographs, and
  published or accepted academic papers.
- Chinese text: SimSun, 12 pt.
- English text: Times New Roman, 12 pt.
- Each item starts on a new line with 2-character indentation.
- Leave one blank line between categories.
- Bold the author's name in published-paper entries.
- Use reference-list formatting as the model for publication entries.

## Acknowledgements

- Heading: `致  谢`, with two spaces between characters; centered; SimHei,
  16 pt, bold; 18 pt before; 12 pt after.
- Leave one blank line after the heading.
- Body: first-line indent 2 Chinese characters; exactly 20 pt line spacing.
- Content should be factual and avoid exaggeration or vulgar wording.

## Header and page numbers

- Page headers begin at the main text and continue to the end of the thesis.
- Header text: SimSun, 10.5 pt.
- Header left side: `中南大学博（或硕）士学位论文`.
- Header right side: chapter number and chapter title, with one space between
  number and title, e.g. `第1章 章名`.
- Page number footer: centered; Times New Roman, 9 pt.
- Front matter from the Chinese abstract through symbol explanation uses
  separate Roman page numbers.
- Main text and back matter use continuous Arabic page numbers starting from the
  first chapter.

## Quantity and unit requirements

- Follow the national standards for quantities and units, including GB3100-93,
  GB3101-93, and GB3102.1-13-93.
- Use either international symbols or Chinese unit names consistently throughout
  the thesis.
- Use upright or italic symbols consistently according to disciplinary practice.

## Printing and cover production

- Use A4 output and duplex printing.
- Doctoral and master thesis covers must follow the school's unified format.
- Doctoral cover main color: deep red.
- Master cover main color: deep blue.
- Recommended cover production: edit the official cover attachment directly and
  print in color on white coated paper.
- Classified theses must follow `中南大学涉密研究生与涉密学位论文管理实施细则`
  (`中大研字〔2021〕7号`).

## Template implementation checklist

When making a DOCX template for this standard:

- Define Word styles for every repeated role: cover fields, title page fields,
  originality statement, Chinese abstract title/body/labels, English abstract
  title/body/labels, TOC heading/entries, abbreviation/symbol tables, body text,
  Heading 1-4, figure captions, table captions, table body, formula, note,
  references, appendix, achievements, acknowledgements, header, and footer.
- Use real Word sections for no-page-number front pages, Roman-number front
  matter, and Arabic-number body/back matter.
- Insert a TOC field instead of a manually typed contents list when possible.
- Insert page-number fields in footers.
- Use a header table or equivalent alignment so the left thesis label and right
  chapter field align cleanly.
- Use a StyleRef field for the chapter title in the running header when possible.
- Build table examples as real three-line tables with explicit borders and
  repeated header rows.
- Include placeholders for medical-only abbreviation and review sections so users
  can keep or delete them.
- Include placeholders for optional symbol explanations and appendices.
- Include a short in-document style inventory page only if the user asks for an
  instructional template; otherwise keep the template as close to a thesis as
  possible.
